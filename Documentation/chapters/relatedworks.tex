\chapter{Related Works}

The peer-to-peer vehicle rental industry has witnessed significant development over the past decade, with several platforms pioneering the shared mobility concept. This chapter examines existing solutions, their approaches, strengths, and limitations, providing context for RideShareX's development.

\section{Commercial Platforms}
Turo, Getaround, and Zipcar represent different approaches to car sharing and peer-to-peer rentals. While these platforms demonstrate feasibility, they often charge high commissions, require specialized hardware, or operate primarily in limited geographies.

\section{Academic and Open Source Projects}
Academic projects and open-source initiatives have explored vehicle sharing concepts, but many remain limited in scope, lack production-ready features (secure authentication, payment integration), or are not actively maintained. RideShareX builds on these insights to provide a localized, full-stack demonstration.

\section{Gap Analysis}
Existing works often lack localization, affordable fee structures, and clear end-to-end workflows. RideShareX targets these gaps by providing a localized MERN-based solution with Google OAuth, a demo payment pipeline, role-based access control, and responsive UI design.
